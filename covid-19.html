Pandemic Best Practice and Protocols for Health and Safety COVID-19

Our priority is the safety of our employees and customers. We will continue to provide peace of mind for our customers. In the event additional measures are in place we will keep you updated.

Our Central Monitoring Station employees are all considered to be designated employees classified as “essential employees.” Essential employees are noted as Exempt and permitted to travel to and from work.

At this time Tel-Tec Security staff members are encouraged to abide by the CDC and OSHA guidelines to continue to work in a safe manner in accordance with the best practices listed below.

  1. Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially after going to the bathroom; before eating; and after blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing.
  2. Cover your cough or sneeze with a tissue, then throw the tissue in the trash, or cough or sneeze into your elbow rather than your hands.
  3. Avoid direct contact with people who are visibly sick or express they are sick or suffer from flu like symptoms.
  4. If, upon arrival at the customer premises, the customer acknowledges that they are affected, then you must contact Tel-Tec Security immediately to have the work rescheduled.
  5. On arrival at the job ask the customer if they have any Pandemic Plan procedures you need to follow.
  6. During the visit, try to maintain a social distancing of 6 feet where possible.
  7. Carry hand sanitizer in your vehicles and use after all personal interactions and after using gas pumps, touching door handles, handrails or other surfaces.
  8. Use disinfectant wipes to wipe down your steering wheel, radio, tools and other surfaces you touch on a DAILY basis.
  9. Also, make sure you are regularly cleaning your pens, cell phone and iPad etc.
  10. Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands.
  11. Use only your knuckle to touch light switches, elevator buttons etc.
  12. Clean your personal desk areas and door handles in area daily

Your reponsibility is the following

  1. To report any travel your employees may have had within the last 14 days to impacted areas or outside of the US. The website link attached gives representation of graphic hotspots of the virus as a reference. https://hgis.uw.edu/virus/
  2. To report any diagnosis of COVID-19 from any of your employees, along with a history of customer sites they may have visited within the last two weeks.
  3. To report if you are experiencing any respiratory illness or have been diagnosed with COVID-19 since you will not be able to perform any work for Tel-Tec Security until you are symptom free for a period of 24 hours without the use of medicines. (e.g. aspirin, fever seducers and/or cough suppressants)
  4. To report if you have come into contact with anyone that has been exposed to the COVID-19, as you will not be permitted to perform any work at our customer locations for 14 days.
  5. If during this 14 day isolation period you experience symptoms you will not be able to perform any work for Tel-Tec Security until you are symptom free for a period of 24 hours without the use of medicines. (e.g. aspirin, fever reducers and/or cough suppressants)
  6. To report if you have had any customers turn you away, location is closed or areas you normally service are being placed in a state of isolation at this time.
  7. To report any other issues, you have come across that impact work being completed.
